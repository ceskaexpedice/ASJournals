Open Access 

 

Since 2017, the journal is also available in the Open Access database, with the printed and electronic versions being the same.

Open access to scientific information is defined as ‘permanent and free online access to documents (in particular full texts) for all users. This status includes free and unlimited reading, downloading, copying, sharing, saving, printing and hypertext linking.’ Nevertheless, Open Access as a movement is almost exclusively focused on the publication of scientific peer-reviewed articles or scientific books.

The Czech Academy of Sciences signed the Berlin Declaration on Open Access to Knowledge in the Sciences and Humanities in the spring of 2008 as the first institution from the Czech Republic. In September 2010, the Academic Council of the CAS approved the Open-Access Policy at the CAS. In 2016, the CAS also signed the Expression of Interest of the OA2020 initiative.
